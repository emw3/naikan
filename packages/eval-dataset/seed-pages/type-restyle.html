Why visual regressions slip through review

By J. Okafor · 6 min read

Most teams catch logic bugs with unit tests, but the rendered page is a different surface. A diff of 0.4% can be anti-aliasing, a ticking clock, or a genuinely broken nav bar — and a tired reviewer treats them alike.

The cost of noise

When every run flags a diff, the diffs stop meaning anything. Teams mute the checks within a few sprints, and the one real regression sails into production behind a wall of false positives.

A second opinion

The fix is not more pixels — it is judgment. A reviewer that explains why a diff is or isn't a regression turns a noisy signal into a trusted one.
